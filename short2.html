Easter Egg Hunt to be held Sunday

by Wade Young

Grab your Easter baskets! The annual Easter Egg Hunt is Sunday.

The egg hunt that features fun and family time is open to anyone up to age 110 years old. It will be held at the American Legion Club from 1 to 3 p.m. There will be egg hunts for groups 0 to 3 years old, 4 to 7 years old, 8 to 10 years old, and 11 to 12 years old.

As in year’s past, complimentary hot dogs, buns, chips and lemonade will be served to everyone. There will also be games, face

painting, pictures with the Easter Bunny and drawings for prizes.

The event is being coordinated by the staff at CornerStone State Bank, and being sponsored by the bank and more than 50 area sponsors. Montgomery branch president, Connie White Tupy said the event is free to everyone who attends.

White-Tupy and her staff resuscitated the hunt five years ago from the brink of extinction, and it’s been growing ever since. In a letter to potential sponsors, she made clear the hunt wouldn’t continue without the community’s support. “Five years ago, we took this on because there wasn’t going to be an Easter Egg Hunt. We do not feel this is a function of the bank, so we need your help,” she wrote. “Without your donations to help defray the costs of the food, prizes, Easter Eggs, etc. we will not be able to continue to do this.”

Joining White-Tupy and her staff will be some members of the Montgomery Chamber of Commerce who expressed interest in possibly organizing the event in the future.
